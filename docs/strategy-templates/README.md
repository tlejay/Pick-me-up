# Strategy Templates for PICK-ME-UP

This directory contains strategic planning templates designed to work with the **venture-strategist** AI agent. These templates help transform business ideas into executable plans.

---

## Available Templates

### 1. LEAN_CANVAS.md
**Purpose:** Validate business model using Lean Canvas framework

**When to Use:**
- Starting a new project or feature
- Validating business assumptions
- Pitching to stakeholders or investors
- Strategic planning sessions

**Key Sections:**
- Problem & Customer Segments
- Solution & Unique Value Proposition
- Channels & Revenue Streams
- Cost Structure & Key Metrics
- Unfair Advantage

---

### 2. MVP_DEFINITION.md
**Purpose:** Define and scope your Minimum Viable Product

**When to Use:**
- Planning initial product launch
- Scoping feature releases
- Prioritizing development work
- Resource planning

**Key Sections:**
- MVP Vision & Goals
- User Stories & Acceptance Criteria
- Feature Prioritization Matrix
- Scope Definition (In/Out)
- Launch Criteria & Timeline

---

### 3. UNIT_ECONOMICS.md
**Purpose:** Analyze business viability through unit economics

**When to Use:**
- Evaluating business model sustainability
- Pricing decisions
- Fundraising preparation
- Growth planning

**Key Sections:**
- Revenue Model Analysis
- CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
- LTV (Lifetime Value)
- LTV:CAC Ratio
- Contribution Margin
- Break-even Analysis
- Scenario Planning

---

### 4. PRODUCT_ROADMAP.md
**Purpose:** Plan product evolution over time

**When to Use:**
- Quarterly/annual planning
- Communicating product vision
- Aligning team priorities
- Stakeholder updates

**Key Sections:**
- Vision & Strategic Pillars
- Roadmap Timeline (by phase)
- Feature Prioritization (RICE)
- Dependencies & Risks
- Success Criteria by Phase

---

### 5. FEATURE_PRIORITIZATION.md
**Purpose:** Systematically prioritize features and initiatives

**When to Use:**
- Sprint planning
- Backlog grooming
- Resource allocation
- Scope negotiations

**Key Sections:**
- RICE Scoring Framework
- Value vs. Complexity Matrix
- MoSCoW Prioritization
- Feature Evaluation Template
- Decision Framework

---

### 6. MEETING_PREP.md
**Purpose:** Prepare for critical stakeholder meetings

**When to Use:**
- Investor pitches
- Board meetings
- Client demos
- Sprint reviews
- Kickoff meetings

**Key Sections:**
- Meeting Objectives & Agenda
- Key Messages & Talking Points
- Demo Plan (if applicable)
- Decisions Required
- Q&A Preparation
- Follow-up Plan

---

### 7. BUSINESS_MODEL_VALIDATION.md
**Purpose:** Validate business model assumptions with data

**When to Use:**
- After MVP launch
- Before scaling investment
- Fundraising preparation
- Pivot decisions

**Key Sections:**
- Value Proposition Validation
- Customer Segment Validation
- Channel Validation
- Revenue Model Validation
- Cost Structure Validation
- Retention & Engagement
- Go/No-Go Decision

---

### 8. ACTION_PLAN.md
**Purpose:** Convert strategy into concrete action items

**When to Use:**
- After strategic planning
- Sprint/quarter kickoff
- Translating vision to execution
- Weekly/monthly planning

**Key Sections:**
- Strategic Priorities
- Action Items by Priority
- Weekly/Sprint Plans
- Resource Allocation
- Milestones & Checkpoints
- Risk Management
- Metrics Dashboard

---

## How to Use These Templates

### 1. Choose the Right Template
Match your current need to the template purpose above.

### 2. Work with venture-strategist Agent
The AI agent can:
- Help you fill out templates
- Provide strategic guidance
- Challenge assumptions
- Suggest priorities
- Identify risks

### 3. Iterate and Refine
These are living documents. Update them as you:
- Learn new information
- Validate assumptions
- Adjust strategy
- Hit milestones

### 4. Share with Team
Templates facilitate:
- Alignment across team
- Clear communication
- Informed decision-making
- Accountability

## Best Practices

### 1. Start Simple
Don't fill every section on day one. Start with the essentials.

### 2. Make it a Habit
Review and update templates regularly (weekly/monthly).

### 3. Involve the Team
Strategy works best when the team is involved in creation.

### 4. Use Data
Back up assertions with data wherever possible.

### 5. Stay Flexible
Templates are guides, not rigid rules. Adapt to your needs.

### 6. Link Documents
Reference related templates to maintain consistency.

### 7. Version Control
Keep track of changes and decisions over time.
